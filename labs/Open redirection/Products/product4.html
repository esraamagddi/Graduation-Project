It's All Just A Click Away

Sam Sandwich | 03 March 2023

What I love most about Social Media is how it fills my days. Time just evaporates with every word I read, every video I watch and every pointless self-analyzing quiz I take part in. I used to tell people I NEED social media to promote my work, that's not to say I NEEDED to spend so much time trawling through my newsfeed and weeping over cute, but heartbreaking, tales of woe. But it sucks you in. Big time. I have 210 friends on Facebook, most of whom I have met in real life. Yet, when I had major surgery in the summer I had zero visitors, messages from 2 people asking after me, and one bunch of flowers from my landlady. I'm pretty sure at one time or another we've all made that life-changing decision to 'come off all social media', it should be up there with all the 'bers', Movember, Stoptober, stopwastingyourlifeonthecomoputerber. You never really leave though, your account stays just as it was and you can log back in any time. And we all do, might be a week or only an hour, but we all do. I'd love to be one of those people that don't (and has never) used social media, I think it makes them cool (or very old). I'm a bit of a voyeur on Facebook, scrolling through, never commenting, in an attempt to avoid all the notifications for the popular posters posts. How is it that someone can bleat on about having a cold, or tummy ache, or some other mundane thing and get 50 likes and multiple comments like, 'chin up love', 'sorry to hear that hun', 'thinking of you babe.' I had major surgery! I was nobody's 'hun', 'love', or 'babe'. It takes all sorts to make a world, I think I'll just stick to the licorice kind from now on.
